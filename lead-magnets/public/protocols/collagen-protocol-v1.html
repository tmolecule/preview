The Morning Ritual

Real caffeine, real spice, real cofactor — sustained, not jittery.

Spiced Chai · Collagen Blend

Tea base
Black tea (Assam or Darjeeling), 1 heaping tsp milled leaf or 1 tea bag
Spices
Cardamom (2–3 pods crushed), cinnamon (1/4 tsp), ginger (1 thin slice fresh)
Water
8–10 oz, just off the boil
Steep
4–5 minutes, covered
Add
10g collagen peptides + 1/4 lemon (or 1 tsp camu camu / acerola powder)
Optional
1 tsp ghee or coconut oil for sustained energy curve

Why this combination

  • Black tea brings the L-theanine that smooths caffeine release — alert without the spike.
  • Cardamom and cinnamon support digestive fire (agni) and have shown blood-sugar steadying effects.
  • Lemon is the vitamin C cofactor that lets the collagen do its work.
  • Ginger warms circulation — Ayurveda recommends it before noon, not after.

Tip: stir the collagen in last, after the brew has rested 60–90 seconds. It mixes more cleanly and the taste blends in.

The Evening Ritual

A second dose, a quieter cup — designed for the sleep window.

Rooibos · Rose Hip · Collagen

Tea base
Rooibos (caffeine-free), 1 heaping tsp
Botanicals
Rose hip (1 tsp dried) and a pinch of hibiscus for vitamin C
Water
8–10 oz, freshly boiled
Steep
5–7 minutes
Add
5g collagen peptides, stirred in once the brew has cooled slightly
Optional
1 tsp raw honey (added off heat) for taste and a small glycine boost

Why this combination

  • Rooibos contains aspalathin, an antioxidant that does not interfere with sleep.
  • Rose hip and hibiscus are two of the most concentrated plant sources of vitamin C — your evening cofactor.
  • Honey + glycine pairing may support deeper, more restorative sleep stages (anecdotal but well-loved).
  • The split dose keeps blood amino acid levels topped up overnight, when growth hormone naturally pulses.

What to add. What to avoid.

The small adjustments that move the score from a B to an A.

Add

  • A vitamin C source with every dose — citrus, hibiscus, rose hip, camu camu, or a 250mg supplement.
  • A fat source when pairing with curcumin or fat-soluble actives — ghee, coconut oil, full-fat milk.
  • Black pepper any time turmeric is on the table — piperine multiplies curcumin bioavailability.
  • Sleep, sun, and protein. Collagen is a building block — it cannot work in isolation.
  • One extra glass of water per cup of tea — hydration carries everything.

Avoid

  • Coffee within 30 minutes of your collagen dose — tannins bind protein.
  • Stirring collagen into a freshly boiled brew — let it rest a minute. Better mix, better taste.
  • Sugar bombs. Spiking insulin alongside collagen blunts the routine. Honey off-heat is fine.
  • Ginger after 6 PM. Warming spices belong in the morning half.
  • Skipping days. Two weeks of consistency beats four weeks of "when I remember."

A word on patience

The clinical studies that measured skin elasticity at 10g/day saw measurable change at 8 to 12 weeks. Joint comfort endpoints take similar time. You are building, not patching.
